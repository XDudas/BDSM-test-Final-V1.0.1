Chess Master Rules

Basic Rules of Chess

  • Objective: The goal is to checkmate your opponent's king. Checkmate happens when the king is in a position to be captured (in check) and cannot escape from capture.
  • Chessboard: The game is played on an 8x8 grid, with alternating light and dark squares. Each player begins with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns.
  • Starting Position: Each player's pieces are arranged on the two nearest rows to them. The second row is filled with pawns, and the first row is filled with rooks, knights, bishops, queen (on her color), and king.

How Pieces Move

  • King: Moves one square in any direction. The king may not move into check.
  • Queen: Moves any number of squares along a row, column, or diagonal.
  • Rook: Moves any number of squares along a row or column.
  • Bishop: Moves any number of squares diagonally.
  • Knight: Moves in an L-shape: two squares in a row or column and then one square perpendicular. Knights can jump over other pieces.
  • Pawn: Moves one square forward. On its first move, a pawn can move two squares forward. Pawns capture diagonally.

Special Moves

  • Castling: The king moves two squares towards a rook, and the rook moves to the square the king skipped over. Castling is only allowed if neither the king nor the rook has moved, the squares between them are unoccupied, and the king is not in check or moving through check.
  • En Passant: If a pawn moves two squares forward from its starting position and lands beside an opponent's pawn, the opponent can capture it as if it moved only one square. This capture must be done immediately.Promotion: When a pawn reaches the opposite side of the board, it can be promoted to any other piece (except a king), typically a queen.

Check and Checkmate

  • Check: A king is in check if it is under threat of capture. A player must move out of check, block the check, or capture the threatening piece.
  • Checkmate: A king is in checkmate if it is in check and cannot make any move to escape check.
